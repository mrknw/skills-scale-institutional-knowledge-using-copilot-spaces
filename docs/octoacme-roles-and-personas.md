# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## Release Manager

### Role Summary
Release Managers oversee the entire release lifecycle, coordinating between development, QA, and deployment teams to ensure smooth and reliable releases to production.

### Responsibilities
- Plan and schedule releases across development cycles
- Coordinate release activities between development and operations teams
- Ensure all release checklists and quality gates are met before deployment
- Maintain release documentation and communication
- Act as the main liaison between technical teams and stakeholders during releases
- Monitor release metrics and identify areas for process improvement

### Goals
- Execute releases on schedule with minimal disruption
- Ensure zero critical issues in production releases
- Maintain clear communication and visibility throughout the release process
- Continuously improve release efficiency and reliability

### Typical Communication
- Release status updates to stakeholders
- Pre-release coordination meetings with dev, QA, and ops teams
- Post-release retrospectives and incident reviews
- Release notes and deployment announcements

### Interaction Points
- **Project Managers**: Aligns release schedules with project timelines and communicates any release-related risks
- **Developers**: Coordinates code freeze dates, reviews PRs for release readiness, and manages release branches
- **Product Managers**: Ensures feature completeness and validates release scope against roadmap priorities
- **Risk Champion**: Escalates release-related risks and coordinates mitigation plans
- **Communications Lead**: Provides release details for stakeholder communications

---

## Risk Champion

### Role Summary
Risk Champions proactively monitor project risks, facilitate timely escalation, and coordinate risk mitigation activities across teams to minimize project disruptions.

### Responsibilities
- Identify and document project risks throughout the project lifecycle
- Maintain and update the risk register with current status and mitigation plans
- Facilitate regular risk reviews during team syncs and planning sessions
- Escalate high-impact risks to appropriate stakeholders promptly
- Work with teams to develop and implement risk mitigation strategies
- Track risk trends and patterns across projects for organizational learning

### Goals
- Identify risks early before they become issues
- Ensure no critical risk goes unaddressed or unescalated
- Build a culture of proactive risk awareness
- Reduce project disruptions through effective risk management

### Typical Communication
- Risk register updates and reviews
- Risk escalation notices to PM and stakeholders
- Risk mitigation status in team standups
- Risk retrospective analysis after project completion

### Interaction Points
- **Project Managers**: Provides risk updates for status reports and coordinates escalation paths
- **Developers**: Identifies technical risks, implementation blockers, and dependency issues
- **Product Managers**: Assesses business impact of risks and validates mitigation priorities
- **Release Manager**: Flags release-related risks and coordinates go/no-go decisions
- **Communications Lead**: Ensures risk-related updates are communicated to appropriate stakeholders

---

## Communications Lead

### Role Summary
Communications Leads drive consistent, transparent communication to internal and external stakeholders, ensuring everyone stays informed about project status, changes, and decisions.

### Responsibilities
- Develop and maintain communication plans for projects
- Coordinate regular status updates to stakeholders at all levels
- Ensure consistent messaging across teams and stakeholder groups
- Facilitate transparent communication of risks, blockers, and decisions
- Collaborate with PM and technical leads to gather and distribute updates
- Manage communication cadences and stakeholder expectations

### Goals
- Maintain transparent and timely communication with all stakeholders
- Reduce information gaps and miscommunication
- Build stakeholder trust through consistent, accurate updates
- Ensure the right people receive the right information at the right time

### Typical Communication
- Weekly stakeholder status reports
- Project kickoff and milestone announcements
- Incident and change notifications
- Decision logs and meeting summaries
- Cross-team coordination updates

### Interaction Points
- **Project Managers**: Gathers project status, risks, and decisions for stakeholder communications
- **Product Managers**: Aligns messaging on product direction, priorities, and customer impact
- **Developers**: Collects technical updates and translates them for non-technical audiences
- **Release Manager**: Coordinates release announcements and deployment communications
- **Risk Champion**: Communicates risk status and escalations to appropriate stakeholder groups
